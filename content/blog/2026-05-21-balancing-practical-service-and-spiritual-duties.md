<p>The sermon from The River Church delves into a critical balance that early Christians faced: balancing practical service with spiritual duties. This theme is vividly illustrated through the story of Stephen, who was appointed to serve tables but ultimately stood tall for his faith.</p><h2>Church Growth Challenges</h2><p>As churches grow, so do problems. The sermon highlights this tension by referencing a common misconception: many people think that because the church has fantastic fruit and growth, it's problem-free. However, as we see in Acts 6, even thriving communities face challenges.</p><h2>The William Booth Example</h2><p>One of the most compelling examples shared was that of William Booth, who started preaching in places others avoided due to their reputation. He reached out to marginalized communities and preached where society had written people off entirely. This example underscores the importance of not just serving tables but also standing tall for those often overlooked.</p><h2>Protecting Assigned Duties</h2><p>The sermon emphasized protecting what God has called us to do, whether it's preaching or providing practical support. The speaker stressed that prayer and study are foundational; they're not one priority among many but the foundation of everything else we stand on.</p>
