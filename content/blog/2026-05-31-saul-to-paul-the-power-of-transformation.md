<p>Imagine a man so zealous in his beliefs that he persecutes Christians with fervor. This was Saul before his encounter on the road to Damascus (Acts 9:1). In one moment, this self-righteous individual is struck blind and encounters Jesus Christ personally.</p><h2>The Danger of Self-Righteousness</h2><p>During the sermon, we delved into what it means to be self-righteous. It's not just about thinking you're better than others; it’s believing that your own goodness or religious performance makes you spiritually superior (Acts 5:10). This is a dangerous mindset because it closes us off from God and His grace.</p><h2>Divine Interruptions for Transformation</h2><p>The speaker shared his personal story of being knocked down during football due to arrogance. It’s in these moments that we are often humbled, much like Saul was on the road to Damascus. These divine interruptions can be uncomfortable but they lead us closer to God.</p><h2>Compassion Over Pride</h2><p>The call for believers is clear: walk in compassion rather than pride (Acts 9). We need people who preach and teach, showing the world Jesus through their actions and words. Let’s strive to be those individuals that others can see Christ in.</p>
