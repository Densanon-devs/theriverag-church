<p>As Moses stood before the Israelites, he delivered a powerful message that echoes through time. In <em>Deuteronomy 1:6</em>, God says to them, “You have stayed at this mountain long enough.” This call for movement and change is not just historical; it’s also very personal.</p><h2>The Journey of Trusting in Timing</h2><p>Moses reminded the Israelites that their journey from Egypt was supposed to take only 11 days. Yet, due to fear and hesitation, they wandered in the wilderness for 40 years. This story is a stark reminder that we can get stuck in our comfort zones or be held back by doubt.</p><h2>Overcoming Fear Through Faith</h2><p>The speaker encouraged us with these words: “People of God, we cry, we weep, we wipe our tears, but then we trust God.” This is a call to action for all of us. When life feels overwhelming or when opportunities seem too daunting, remember that trusting in God’s timing can lead you out of the wilderness.</p><h2>Embracing New Opportunities</h2><p>The sermon also highlighted how sometimes what happened yesterday might not be fair, but it doesn’t define our future. The speaker shared a personal anecdote: “She said, I’m going to give us 30 minutes.” This moment serves as an invitation for each of us to step into new opportunities with courage and faith.</p><h2>Conclusion</h2><p>The message from Deuteronomy is clear: it’s time to move. Whether you’re facing a transition in your career, relationships, or personal growth, remember that God calls us out of our comfort zones not because He wants to make things difficult but because He has something better for us.</p>
